About Age-Related Macular Degeneration

Age-Related Macular Degeneration (AMD) is a medical condition that affects the macula, the central part of the retina responsible for sharp vision. It can cause blurred or reduced central vision and is a leading cause of vision loss in older adults.

There are two main types:

  • Dry AMD – gradual thinning of the macula.
  • Wet AMD – abnormal blood vessel growth under the retina.

Prevention Tips

Regular Eye Checkups

Routine eye examinations help detect AMD early.

Healthy Diet

Foods rich in vitamins, antioxidants, and omega-3 help maintain retinal health.

Avoid Smoking

Smoking increases the risk of macular degeneration.
